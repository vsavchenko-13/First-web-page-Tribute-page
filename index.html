Howard Hughes

American business magnate, investor, record-setting pilot, engineer, film director, and philanthropist.

1905 -
Hughes was born in Houston, Texas was a son of a succesfull oil-drill tool maker.
1923 -
At the age of 18 inherited family business.
1926 -
Filmed his first movie on the money he saved from family business which featured very expensive aeriel shot.
1930 -
Started his own aircraft company. Besides designing and building airplanes he also risked his life testing them.
1940 -
Hughes Tool Company ventured into film industry when it obtained partial ownership of RKO companies.
1946 -
A terrible plane crash Howard started to retreat from the world, and for a couple of years he was conducting all his business from the top floor of the Desert Inn and only few people ever saw him. It was speculated that he potentially was suffering from a very sever OCD.
1947 -
The biggest flying boat built Hercules H-4 made its first and last flight for one mile, after which it was left in the climate-controlled hangar.
1953 -
Howard launched the Howard Medical Institute in Miamy Florida with the expressed goal of basic biomedical research including tryint to understand in Hughe's words, "the genesis of life itself".
1954 -
Hughes had gained almost full control over RKO for the price of $24 Million.
1972 -
In 1972, during the cold war era, Hughes was approached by the CIA through his longtime partner, David Charnay, to help secretly recover the Soviet submarine K-129, which had sunk near Hawaii four years earlier
1976 -
Howard Hughes dies because of kidney failure while on the plain back to home in Houston. FBI reported that he was so unrecognizable that they had to take fingerprints of his body to identify that it was Howard.
